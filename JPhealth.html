JPealth Foods' App Design

Project Overiew

The Product


JPealth Food is a houston-based restaurant chain. JPealth attempts to provide healthy options to its clients. JPealth caters to consumers who do not have the time to cook their own meals.


The Product Duration


December 2021 - March 2022

The Problem:


Students and busy workers don’t have the time to prepare their own meals

The Goal:


Design a JPealth food app that allows users to order healthy alternatives and have them delivered to their door

My Role:


UX designer designing the JPealth food from start to finish

Responsibilities:


Paper and digital wireframes, low and high fidelity prototyping, conducting interviews and usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs

Understanding the User

User research: summary


To get a better understanding of the consumers I'm designing for and their requirements, I conducted interviews and created empathy maps. Working parents who didn't have time to cook for their family were a main user category found through research.


This user group confirmed initial assumptions about JPealth’s customers but research showed that time wasn’t the only factor stopping users from cooking at home. Other users' problems were the number of people ordering they were ordering for, the ability to get groceries, the menu selection, and if going in person was even viable.

Problem Statement:


Presley is a parent that studies full time who needs the ability to order food because she doesn’t have the time to cook for her entire family.

User journey map

Mapping Presley’s user journey revealed how helpful it would be for users to have access to JPealth Foods’ App

Starting the Design


Paper wireframes


Taking the time to sketch out each screen of the app on paper assured that the digital wireframes would be appropriate for addressing user issues. On the homescreen, Customizability was a top priority for me.

Digital wireframes


As the initial design phase continued, I made sure to base screen designs on feedback and findings from the user research.

Digital wireframes


Ease of navigation was a key user need to address in the designs in addition to equipping the app to work with assistive technologies.

Usability study: findings

Round 1 Findings:


Users needed more space between icons


Deals needed to be added to the navigation bar


Users wanted to have the option to add customer favorites to their order

Round 2 findings:


Users didn’t want to customize their salads and soup


Users didn’t like the old menu

Accessibility considerations

Provided access to users who are vision-impaired through adding alt text to images for screen readers.

Used icons to help make navigation easier.

Used buttons to help make navigating the menu faster

Going forward


Takeaways

Impact


This app inspired users to think about their health options and if there were similar alternatives


One Quote from peer Feedback:

“I can see myself using this application in the future. It made ordering food painless and easy.”

What I learned


While designing the JPealth’s app, I learned that the first idea you get is built upon and shaped by the users into a full product that they will enjoy.

Next Steps

Conduct another round of usability studies to validate whether the pain points users experienced have been effectively addressed.

Add more useful screens, and improve the design of the entire app.

Conduct more user research to determine any new areas of need.
