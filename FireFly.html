Firefly Landing Page and Website

Project Overiew

The Product


Firefly is a rock band from Denver, Colorado. Firefly's primary audience is its listeners.


The Product Duration


Less than a month

The Problem:


Firefly rockband doesn't have a webpage for fans to order tickets

The Goal:


Design a Firefly Website that allows users to order tickets for their newest shoing.

My Role:


UX designer designing the Firefly website from start to finish

Responsibilities:


Paper and digital wireframes, low and high fidelity prototyping, conducting interviews and usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs

Understanding the User

User research: summary


To get a better understanding of the consumers I'm designing for and their requirements, I conducted interviews and created user journey maps. Fans who didn't have a way to reliably order tickets were the main user category found through research.


This user group confirmed initial assumptions about Firefly's fans and research showed that the website was the only factor stopping users from ordering online.

Problem Statement:


Derrick is a parent with a busy job who needs the ability to order tickets fast because he doesn't have that much time.

User journey map

To come up with suggestions for how to fix the holes revealed in the competitive audit, I undertook a short ideation exercise. My focus was specifically on enabling users to quickly enroll in classes.

Starting the Design


Paper wireframes


Taking the time to sketch out each screen of the app on paper assured me that the digital wireframes would be appropriate for addressing user issues. From the beginning, I knew that I had wanted a hero image on the landing page so I had ideated with that in mind.

Digital wireframes


As the initial design phase continued, I made sure to base screen designs on feedback and findings from the user research.

Low-fidelity prototype


Using the completed set of digital wireframes, I created a low-fidelity prototype. The primary user flow I connected with was booking a ticket to their newest concert.


Firefly's low-fidelity prototype can be found here.

Usability study: findings

Round 1 Findings:


A drop-down list is needed so that users could change the number of tickets they intended to order.

Round 2 findings:


Users felt more comfortable with the menu at the top

Accessibility considerations

Provided access to users who are visually impaired through adding alt text to images for screen readers

Used icons to help make navigation easier.

Used buttons to help make navigating the menu faster.

Going forward


Takeaways

Impact


This website lets users schedule from firefly without having to go to a third-party vendor.


One Quote from peer Feedback:

“I love that this band has a website. It makes me feel like the artist has a connection with me.”

What I learned


While working on the Firefly website, I realized that information targeted at a specific demographic should have a page dedicated to that demographic rather than a website that caters to the whole public.

Next Steps

Conduct another round of usability studies to validate whether the pain points users experienced have been effectively addressed.

Add more useful screens, and improve the design of the website

Conduct more user research to determine any new areas of need
