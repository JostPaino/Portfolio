HelloSuccess APP and Responsive Website

Project Overiew

The Product


HelloSuccess is a Houston-based financial education organization. The organization needs a tool to help people understand and manage their finances. Hello Success' primary target audience is college students and high school grads who are just starting out in the workforce


The Product Duration


less than a month

The Problem:


Every year, the number of young adults on the planet increases. The HelloSuccess strategy team has discovered that there is a lack of general financial knowledge, and that this lack of understanding leads to unpleasant banking experiences and debt that can last for years.

The Goal:


Create an application that will improve the lives of users by teaching them about financial stability and money management.

My Role:


UX designer designing the app and responsive website design from conception to delivery.

Responsibilities:


Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, iterating on designs, determining information architecture, and responsive design.

Understanding the User

User research: summary


I developed interview questions based on HelloSuccess' data, which I then used to conduct user interviews. Most interviewees said they were worried about how they'd manage their money in a few years, but they weren't doing anything different to improve their situation. Users would be willing to take a course on their own time if they had access to an easy-to-use tool to teach them when they needed it, according to user input gathered through research.

Problem Statement:


Ryker is a night shift store manager who needs the flexibility to take classes on his own time because his current schedule doesn't provide him with many options.

Ideation


To come up with suggestions for how to fix the holes revealed in the competitive audit, I undertook a short ideation exercise. My focus was specifically on enabling users to quickly enroll in classes.

Starting the Design


Design System


I created a design framework for this case study to make sure the entire application had a more consistent look.

Digital wireframes


After ideating and drafting some paper wireframes, I created the initial designs for the HelloSuccess app. These designs centered on providing users with individualized guidance to assist them in deciding where to focus their studies next.

Low-fidelity prototype


I designed a low-fidelity prototype to prepare for usability testing that integrated the user flow of entering information into the search bar with filters, registering in the class, and viewing what it has to offer.


View HelloSuccess’ Low-fidelity prototype here.

Usability study: parameters

Study type:

Unmoderated usability study

Location:

Houston, remote

Participants:

10 participants

Length:

30-60 minutes

Usability study: findings


These were the main findings uncovered by the usability study:

Search


People struggled to navigate the catalog using only a filter.

Course information


People preferred to see course information in a pop-up rather than on the same line as the photograph.

Layout


People struggled to click the buttons if they were close together.

Refining the Design

Mockups

I made design improvements based on the findings of the usability research, such as making the learning paths scroll sideways so that users can browse learning paths without unintentionally clicking on others.

Additional design adjustments included the addition of a search box that allowed users to search the platform for the course they needed.

Mockups

High-fidelity Prototype

The high-fidelity prototype followed the same user flow as the low-fidelity prototype, with design improvements made in response to the usability study.

Accessibility considerations

Courses on the home page let customers quickly find the course or type of activity they want to participate in.

Clear labels for interactive elements that can be read by screen readers

Responsive Design


Sitemap

After finishing the app designs, I began work on the responsive website. To ensure a unified and consistent experience across devices, I used the HelloSuccess sitemap to guide the organizational structure of each screen's design.

Responsive designs

The designs for screen size variation included mobile, tablet, and desktop. I optimized the designs to fit specific user needs of each device and screen size.

Going forward


Takeaways

Impact


Users shared that the app would help them be more financially secure.


One Quote from peer feedback was that:

"The HelloSuccess app helps offer financial education to the Houston community in a way that's straightforward and entertaining."

What I learned


I realized that, despite the magnitude of the challenge I was attempting to tackle, meticulously following each phase of the design process and aligning with particular user needs helped me come up with solutions that were both viable and beneficial.

Next Step

Conduct research on how confident people are after using the app.

Provide in-app live events for users to have an additional way to learn information

Conduct another round of usability studies to validate whether the pain points users experienced have been effectively addressed.
