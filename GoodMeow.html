Good Meow-ning App Design with Complimentary Landing Page


Project Overiew

The Product


Good Meow-ning takes pride in being a one-stop-shop for all things cat and coffee. They need a new home page and a mobile app that allows customers to place orders quickly and easily.


The Product Duration


1 week

The Problem:


Their current consumer base would prefer a mobile application because they can't use a PC when they are on the go.

The Goal:


Create an application and landing page that will provide users with the ability to order quickly and effectively.

My Role:


UX designer designed the mobile application and complimentary website from start to finish.

Responsibilities:


Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and, iterating on designs.

Understanding the User


User research: summary


I used Good Meow-ning's data to create interview questions, which I then used to conduct user interviews. The majority of those surveyed indicated they'd like to be able to place orders fast while on the go.

Problem Statement:


Vivian is a produce manager who needs the ability to order coffee remotely because she doesn't have the time to come inside to order.

Big Picture Storyboard

I designed a storyboard to record Vivian's user experience and imagine how the application would impact her life.

Ideation


I did a quick brainstorming exercise to come up with options for how to solve Vivian's pain points. My main emphasis was on making it easy for consumers to place orders fast and efficiently.

Starting the Design


Design System


I created a design framework for this case study to make sure the entire application had a more consistent look.

Digital wireframes


After ideating and drafting some paper wireframes, I created the initial designs for the Good Meow-ning app. These designs centered on providing users with the ability to order quickly and effectively while seeing featured items.

Low-fidelity prototype


I designed a low-fidelity prototype to prepare for usability testing that integrated the user flow of choosing the type of coffee, editing the order, and seeing the items added to the cart.


View Good Meow-ning's Low-fidelity prototype here

Usability study: parameters

Study type:

moderated usability study

Location:

Houston, remote

Participants:

5 participants

Length:

30-40 minutes

Usability study: findings


These were the main findings uncovered by the usability study:

Customization


Lack of order customization impacted the overall user experience

Search


Having search enabled on every page allows users to quickly go into a different item

Refining the Design


Mockups

I made design improvements based on the findings of the usability research, such as making the menu scroll sideways so that users view particular items in the menu and adding a button that displays how many products are in the cart.

Instead of buttons, a collapsible section got added to the design. These sections would provide users with extra options when creating their orders.

Mockups

High-fidelity Prototype

The high-fidelity prototype followed the same user flow as the low-fidelity prototype, with design improvements made in response to the usability study.


View Good Meow-ning's High-fidelity prototype here.

Accessibility considerations

The navigation menu on the home screen lets users quickly find their way throughout the application.

Clear labels for interactive elements that can be read by screen readers

Going forward


Takeaways

Impact


Users shared that the app would help them order when they have time constraints.

One Quote from peer feedback was that:


"The Good Meow-ning app helps me order my favorite coffee when I want and it's so much better than having to call in."

What I learned


I built a design system that worked for me using specific aspects I wanted to see repeated and perhaps adjusted when I first started. It was my first time adopting such a method, and just doing that decreased the time I spent designing. It was game-changing to be able to update and reuse some elements while maintaining the same dimension, color, or text styles.

Next Step

Conduct research on how confident people are after using the app.

Add more useful screens, and improve the design of the entire app.

Conduct another round of usability studies to validate whether the pain points users experienced have been effectively addressed.
